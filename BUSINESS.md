# Business Ideas

## Future Business Models

These are possible business models to consider after the MVP proves demand. They are not part of the current MVP unless explicitly moved into scope.

### Sponsored Local Recommendations

Charge businesses to promote relevant experiences on the platform.

Potential customer categories:

- Restaurants
- Bars and lounges
- Cafes
- Event organizers
- Travel and tourism businesses
- Experience providers
- Date-night venues

Possible models:

- Sponsored placement in generated date plans.
- Sponsored placement in browsable date catalogs.
- Pay-per-click for venue profile visits.
- Pay-per-booking or pay-per-reservation.
- Local business subscription for ongoing visibility.
- Seasonal promotion packages for events or holidays.

Why it could matter:

- Creates revenue beyond one-time user purchases.
- Aligns with businesses that already pay for local discovery.
- Could make the catalog and current-events features more valuable.
- Helps monetize high-intent users who are actively deciding where to go.

Risks:

- Sponsored recommendations can weaken trust if they feel less thoughtful.
- Paid placement must be clearly disclosed.
- The app should not recommend a business only because it paid.
- Requires quality controls so promoted venues still match the user's inputs.
- Adds sales, operations, and local-market complexity.

Principle:

> Sponsored businesses can pay for visibility, but the product must protect user trust by only showing promotions that fit the date plan.
