# Marketing Notes

## Brand

App name: Amora

Domain: `planwithamora.com`

Tagline:

> Date plans with intention.

Primary CTA:

> Plan with Amora.

Rationale:

- One-word name that feels soft, romantic, elegant, and memorable.
- Avoids the direct Courted naming conflict found during quick competitor/domain research.
- The domain clarifies the product action while keeping the app name simple.
- "Plan with Amora" makes the brand feel like a tool for thoughtful planning, not another person taking credit for the date.

Copy watchout:

- Avoid phrasing that makes Amora sound like another woman helped him plan the date.
- Prefer "planned with Amora", "plan with Amora", or "he used Amora to plan around what she likes."
- Avoid "Amora helped him plan this" in influencer scripts.

## Primary Audience

Start with single men.

Target user:

- Single men, roughly 24-38.
- Already have a date or are trying to plan one.
- Want to come across thoughtful without spending hours researching.
- Willing to pay a small premium to avoid fumbling the date.

Reasoning:

- The pain is sharper than for men already in relationships.
- Planning anxiety is higher before or during early dating.
- The outcome feels urgent and high-stakes.
- A $4.99 unlock can feel small compared with the cost of the date.

Men in relationships can still use the app, but relationship-maintenance positioning should come later.

## Audience Scope

MVP marketing focuses on men planning dates for women.

This is an intentional go-to-market constraint, not a permanent product limitation. It fits the first channel: women dating-advice creators giving men advice on how to plan better dates.

The product and data model should stay gender-neutral where practical so future positioning can expand to women planning for men, queer couples, married couples, and other relationship contexts without a rewrite.

## Core Positioning

The app helps men plan dates that feel thoughtful, personal, and intentional.

Primary message:

> Plan a date that makes her feel seen.

Supporting messages:

- Tell us what she likes. We will build a thoughtful 3-stop date plan near you.
- Built around what she likes, your budget, and places nearby.
- Show her you actually thought this through.
- Make the money you are already spending on the date worth it by planning something that helps her feel seen.
- A better plan turns dinner, dessert, and travel into effort she can actually notice.

Avoid copy that implies manipulation, control, or guaranteed romantic outcomes.

## Stand Out Angle

Use distinctiveness through effort as a secondary marketing pillar.

This angle speaks to the masculine desire to be memorable, competent, and different from the average guy without sounding bitter or competitive.

Core idea:

> A thoughtful date is how you stand out.

Supporting messages:

- "Most guys pick dinner and drinks. Be the guy who actually plans something."
- "If every other guy asks 'what do you want to do?', show up with a plan."
- "Different does not mean expensive. It means intentional."
- "The bar is low. A real plan makes you memorable."
- "Want to stand out? Plan like you paid attention."

Use this as the male ego hook beside the primary emotional promise:

- Primary emotional promise: make her feel seen.
- Male ego hook: stand out by actually planning.

## Influencer Channel

The strongest early channel is women dating-advice creators who speak to men.

Why this works:

- These creators already have the trust bridge.
- Men follow them to learn what women actually notice.
- The creator can frame the app as effort made easier.
- The product demo is visual and easy to understand.

Creator content angles:

- "Men, stop asking 'what do you want to do?'"
- "If he planned a date like this, I would notice."
- "Green flag: he remembers what you like and plans around it."
- "I tested this AI date planner for men and it actually made a thoughtful plan."
- "Women can tell when you actually planned something."

Creator demo flow:

1. Enter what she likes.
2. Pick budget, vibe, duration, and no-drinking preference.
3. Show the anonymized preview.
4. Unlock the full plan.
5. React to why the exact plan would feel thoughtful.

TikTok creative example:

- Visual: Girl holding a man's arm, face tucked into his arm, with trending romantic music.
- On-screen text: "He confessed he used an app to plan this date and honestly it made me like him more because he actually put in effort."
- Caption angle: "Effort is attractive. Send this to someone who needs a little help planning."
- Product beat: Show that the app helped him turn what she likes into a thoughtful plan, not that it replaced the effort.

## Woman-To-Man Sharing Loop

Women can be marketed to as the distribution channel, even if men are the paying users.

Woman-facing message:

> If he says he wants to plan something but does not know what to do, send him this.

Share angles:

- "Send this to the guy who keeps saying 'I'm down for whatever.'"
- "Normalize men planning dates that are actually thoughtful."
- "If he wanted to plan something cute but needs help, this is the help."
- "A low-effort man will not use it. A thoughtful man will."
- "POV: you told him you like bookstores once and he plans a whole date around it."
- "Ladies, stop doing the emotional labor of planning the date. Send him this."

Potential CTA:

- Send this to him.
- Drop the hint.
- Make planning easy for him.

The MVP does not need a separate women-facing product experience. It only needs marketing copy that women can share with men.

## Conversion Strategy

The free preview should prove relevance without leaking the exact plan.

Preview shows:

- Anonymized 3-stop concepts.
- Matched-interest signals.
- Budget, duration, and no-drinking badges.
- Enough specificity to build trust.

Preview hides:

- Venue names.
- Addresses.
- Exact timing.
- Full reasons.
- Detailed cost estimate.
- Apple Maps actions.

Paywall:

- Product: Unlock 1 Thoughtful Date Plan.
- Product id: `thoughtful_date_plan_unlock_1`.
- Target price: $4.99.
- Paid unlock reveals the exact 3-stop plan and includes 1 exact-plan regenerate.

Conversion thesis:

- The preview builds trust.
- The paywall sells specificity and confidence.
- The unlock price should feel cheap compared with the date, but premium compared with generic date ideas.
- The purchase protects the larger date spend by helping the user plan something more thoughtful.
